# Serenity Research Posts

## Overview

Use this skill to turn rough notes into rigorous, high-signal market research posts about AI infrastructure, semiconductors, hardware supply chains, bottlenecks, and derivative trade setups.

Do not impersonate Serenity, claim access to Serenity's private views, or copy source posts. Use the public samples only as a structural reference for original analysis.

## Workflow

1. Identify the post type:
   - `hidden-bottleneck thesis`: a ticker or asset benefits from a demand signal the market may not have priced.
   - `research-method proof`: explain why a prior call or framework was unusually strong.
   - `credibility / social proof`: react to press, third-party validation, or a milestone.
   - `short signal`: compress the thesis into a concise X post.
2. Ask for missing facts only when the draft would otherwise require invented data. For market cap, revenue, price, options, estimates, or time-sensitive news, verify current data or clearly label user-provided figures with an "as of" date.
3. Build the evidence chain:
   - observed demand or behavior shift
   - technical mechanism that explains why the demand exists
   - direct beneficiary or pass-through instrument
   - financial materiality relative to company size or segment revenue
   - why the market may be ignoring or mispricing it
   - downside risks, constraints, and position-sizing caveats
4. Draft in an analytical, ticker-dense, informal voice. Keep paragraphs short. Use bullets for mechanism, numbers, and risks.
5. Close with a TLDR when the post is longer than a few paragraphs.

## Output Rules

- Prefer concrete tickers, products, supply-chain nodes, component names, end markets, and mechanisms over broad hype.
- Anchor claims with numbers whenever possible: market cap, revenue, gross margin, segment mix, consensus growth, option maturity, volatility, shipment volume, or unit economics.
- Separate observation from inference. Phrases such as "appears", "if this continues", "non-zero chance", and "main downside risk" are useful when uncertainty matters.
- Include at least one bear-case or risk paragraph for trade ideas.
- Avoid financial-advice language. Do not tell readers to buy, sell, or size positions.
- Do not fabricate quotes, engagement stats, financials, screenshots, press coverage, or claims of third-party validation.
